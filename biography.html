Biography

3 Brothers is an Irish pop group based in New York City. The band was formed by Blake Chaps (former lead singer of The Frontiers), with Jeremy Kins (of Trackers), and Jeff Ofins (of Snow and Salt Trains). 3 Brothers released two albums: Beyond Us and In another Life in August 2000 and Some Nights in February 2001.

The band is best known for three hit singles from Some Nights: Grammy Award-winning "We just choose to live" (featuring Jon B), "Sometime it works", and "Carry over the time". "Carry over the time" reached number one on the U.K. Billboard Hot 100 and Digital Songs charts. It also peaked at number one in the US. "Some Nights" was released as the album's second single in June 2007, peaking at number three on the Hot 100 chart and becoming 3 Brothers second Top 10 single, as well as the band's second song to reach platinum status in the UK.

3 Brothers's song "Carry over the time" won them Grammy awards for Best New Artist and Song of the Year in 2007. Additionally, 3 Brothers were nominees for four other Grammy Awards: Record of the Year and Best Rock Duo or Group Performance (both for "Sometime it works") along with Album of the Year and Best Rock Vocal Album (both for Some Nights).
